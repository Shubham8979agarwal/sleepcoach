About Me

I hold a Bachelor of Science in Behavioral Neuroscience and Psychology, and I am currently completing my M.D. (Doctor of Medicine). I have also recently completed a certification in CBT-I (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia). During my ongoing medical training, I've worked in numerous hospitals and clinics across various countries. It's become evident to me that many healthcare professionals face significant challenges, such as limited time and resources, when addressing insomnia—a crucial aspect of overall health that often goes unaddressed.

Prior to my medical training, I dedicated many years to teaching, serving as an instructor for piano, and also a math and science teacher for adolescents. The ability to educate individuals about the complexities of sleep, coupled with tailored behavioral interventions, forms a crucial aspect of the recovery process from insomnia

My Journey

I am someone who has personally faced the relentless challenges of insomnia. Enduring sleepless nights throughout my college years, I found myself in a seemingly endless cycle of fatigue and frustration. The futility I felt, the hopelessness of not being able to sleep—it was an agonizing experience. I would have done anything, paid any price, just to experience a night of restful sleep again. It was during this tumultuous period that I embarked on a journey to find a solution. Through my own struggles, I discovered the transformative power of CBT-I. It not only transformed my sleep but also inspired me to help others on their path to restful nights.

Sleep coaching, or sleep consulting, involves personalized guidance and support to help individuals improve their sleep quality. It may include strategies to address insomnia, establish healthy sleep habits, and enhance overall well-being.
If you struggle with sleep issues such as insomnia, erratic sleep patterns, or poor sleep quality, sleep coaching can provide tailored strategies and support to help you achieve more restful nights and improved overall health.

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia (CBT-I) is a structured, evidence-based approach aimed at improving sleep by addressing thoughts, behaviors, and habits that may contribute to insomnia. Unlike medications, CBT-I focuses on developing healthy sleep patterns and changing behaviors that hinder restful sleep. It often involves techniques such as sleep restriction, stimulus control, cognitive restructuring, and relaxation strategies. CBT-I is known for its effectiveness in treating insomnia and is a key component of my sleep coaching approach. If you have specific questions about CBT-I or how it may benefit you, feel free to discuss it during your initial consultation.
